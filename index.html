Market Structure Analysis and Production Function Estimation An Empirical Study of the Seafood Processing Industry in Vietnam

Subject: Industrial Organization

Hover to view Abstract

Abstract

Purpose - This study aims to integrate industrial organization theory with microeconomic production analysis to investigate the determinants of firm performance in Vietnam's seafood processing industry. The primary objective is twofold: to quantify market concentration and to empirically estimate the industry's production function. Design/methodology - The research utilizes an unbalanced panel dataset of 435 seafood processing enterprises from 2012 to 2016. By applying the translog production function and calculating the Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI), the study captures the non-linear relationship between input factors (capital, labor, and raw materials) and output. Findings highlight that while the industry is structurally competitive, scale inefficiencies and capital constraints significantly hinder profitability. Recommendations emphasize technological upgrading and strategic clustering to enhance global competitiveness.

THE IMPACT OF INTERNET PENETRATION ON BANK PROFITABILITY EMPIRICAL EVIDENCE FROM 57 COUNTRIES AND IMPLICATIONS FOR VIETNAM’S COMMERCIAL BANKS (2018 - 2024)

Subject: Digital Economics

Hover to view Abstract

Abstract

This study explores the rationale, objectives, and scope of internet penetration on bank profitability. It examines empirical evidence from 57 countries and provides implications for Vietnam's commercial banks. It investigates transaction cost theory, technology acceptance model, and digital transmission channels that affect ROA metrics across the global banking sector. By utilizing Generalized Method of Moments (GMM) estimators to address potential endogeneity, our findings reveal an inverted U-shaped relationship between internet penetration and profitability. Initially, the high costs of digital infrastructure depress margins; however, crossing a specific threshold of adoption triggers substantial operational cost reductions and enhanced fee-based income, ultimately amplifying profitability for proactive institutions.

APPLICATION OF BLOCKCHAIN TECHNOLOGY IN INTERNATIONAL PAYMENT BY LC OF VIETNAMESE COMMERCIAL BANKS

Subject: International Payment

Hover to view Introduction

Introduction

International trade is the lifeblood of the global economy, and for over a century, the Letter of Credit (L/C) has served as its foundational instrument for risk mitigation. By substituting a bank's creditworthiness for that of an importer, the L/C establishes essential trust between cross-border parties. However, despite its critical role, the underlying mechanics of the traditional L/C remain tethered to archaic, paper-intensive processes that are susceptible to fraud, delays, and exorbitant compliance costs. This research proposes the implementation of Distributed Ledger Technology (DLT), specifically smart contracts via platforms like Hyperledger Fabric, to automate document verification. The findings demonstrate that blockchain application can reduce transaction processing time from 5-10 days to under 24 hours, significantly mitigating human errors and enhancing transparency in Vietnamese commercial banks.

DEMOGRAPHIC AND SOCIO-ECONOMIC DETERMINANTS OF INCOME PER CAPITA IN VIETNAM AN ANALYSIS USING PROVINCIAL DATA IN 2023

Subject: Econometrics 1

Hover to view Abstract

Abstract

This study provides a quantitative analysis of the demographic and socio-economic determinants affecting income per capita in Vietnam. Utilizing an econometrics model with provincial data from 2023, the research aims to uncover correlations and significant factors impacting regional economic development and income distribution across different provinces. The methodology incorporates spatial autoregressive (SAR) models to account for regional spillover effects. Empirical findings underscore that urbanization rates, Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) inflows, and educational attainment levels are the most statistically significant drivers of income per capita. Additionally, the study identifies a growing rural-urban income disparity and suggests targeted policy interventions, focusing on infrastructure development and vocational training in lagging regions, to promote inclusive economic growth.
